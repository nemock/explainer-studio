# PLAYBOOK.md template — the per-project learning record

Every project dir gets a `PLAYBOOK.md`, created at the Blueprint gate and
appended at every stage after. It is the human- and AI-readable record of WHY
this video is the way it is — for learning, reference, and modeling future
videos. Write it for a reader who has never seen this session: no shorthand,
no references to conversation context that isn't on the page.

Maintain it at these moments (append, don't rewrite history):
- after Blueprint approval → §1–3
- after script approval → §4
- after recording → §5 (takes, drift findings, what the operator changed)
- after render/QA → §6
- after publish (when stats exist) → §7 (the Learn loop reads this)

---

```markdown
# Playbook — <video title>

*Project: <dir name> · Format: <16:9 deep dive / 9:16 short> · Status: <stage>*

## 1. The bet
Two or three sentences: what this video is betting on (the angle), and the
evidence that bet is based on. Name the gap in plain language.

## 2. What the competitive sweep taught us
- The finalist table (or a pointer to intel/blueprint.md) with the 2–3 rows
  that mattered most and the one-line lesson each.
- The conventions we are honoring (title pattern, hook shape, length) and why.
- The demand/authenticity signals from comment mining that shaped decisions.

## 3. Decisions and why
Bulleted log of every material decision with its reasoning, e.g.:
- Title chosen + why over runner-up
- Voice tier (operator vs Kokoro) + why
- Duration target + midroll reasoning
- What we deliberately left out, and why

## 4. The script's machinery
- The retention map in prose: where each loop opens/closes, where the
  re-hooks land, where the energy shift is, the ending move.
- Which talk-time positions were adapted, and into which segments.
- Anything a future scriptwriter should copy — or avoid — from this script.

## 5. Production notes
- Recording: retakes worth remembering, ad-lib drift findings, delivery notes.
- Assets: what stock searches worked/failed; artifact-design notes.
- Anything that cost more time than expected.

## 6. Render & QA
- QA warnings and what was done about them; render timings; final duration
  vs. target.

## 7. Results (append when known — the Learn loop reads this section)
- Publish date, title/thumbnail used.
- Views vs. channel baseline at 7/30 days; retention notes; CTR if known.
- Verdict on the bet from §1: did the gap angle work? What does the NEXT
  video on an adjacent topic inherit from this one?
```
