## 2. What the winners share (the conventions to respect)

- **Consensus content.** Every strong video converges on the same six beats: do things that don't scale · founder does sales personally · direct outreach beats every "scalable" channel at the start · sell to early adopters (startups) first · charge from day one · it's a numbers game (track the funnel). A credible video must *include* these — the differentiation is in execution, not contradiction.
- **Titles:** number promise ("first 100", "0 → 1,000+") + a qualifier that removes an objection ("Without Ads", "With ZERO Audience", "no budget") + either borrowed authority (YC) or earned authority ($X MRR). 8–12 words.
- **Hooks (first 30 s):** three proven shapes — negation ("Forget ads, forget SEO…"), credential-stack ("everything I know from $35M ARR"), proof-montage (Høiberg). All promise a *system/playbook*, defer the self-intro, and reach the thesis in <10 s.
- **Thumbnails:** face + 3–5 word number-promise text + red→green before/after contrast ("ZERO ❌ → 1000 ✅") or progression arrow. High-saturation yellow/green/red on dark or grid background.
- **Length:** winners cluster at 9–17 min (the 55-min outlier wins on uniqueness, not format). Sweet spot: **12–15 min** — comfortably past the 8-min midroll threshold.
- **Comment-section law:** "straight to the point, no BS" is the most-repeated praise across all finalists. Density is the retention strategy.

## 3. The gap (our angle)

Four things none of the 12 finalists do:

1. **They all speak from the summit.** Every narrator is post-success ($35M ARR, 60M users, YC partner). The viewer with *zero* customers and *zero* audience can't map the advice back. Nobody owns the "you have no audience, no brand, no network, no money" starting position honestly — they gesture at it in titles, then assume resources in the content.
2. **Nobody shows the arithmetic that kills founders.** YC's funnel-math segment (500 emails → 2 customers; 100 emails → 0 — *and the wrong conclusion founders draw from it*) is the single most counterintuitive, save-worthy insight in 12 videos, and it's buried at minute 16 of a 23-minute lecture. It deserves to be the cold open of its own video.
3. **Advice, not artifacts.** Nobody walks through the actual things: the real prospect spreadsheet, an actual message that got customer #1, a calendar of what week one looks like. (YC shows Brex's 2017 email — nine years stale.)
4. **The 2026 inversion is unclaimed.** Comment sections are already full of AI-outreach spam (astroturf bots were visible in our own pull). Everyone's automated outreach is precisely why manual, researched, do-things-that-don't-scale outreach now *over*-performs — the classic YC advice has gotten *stronger* and nobody has made that argument.

**Recommended angle:** *"The math nobody shows you + the plan that survives it."* A 13-minute deep dive that opens on the funnel arithmetic (why 100 emails ≈ 0 customers and why that's not failure), then delivers a concrete 30-day, artifact-driven operating plan for a founder with no audience — with the 2026 twist (AI spam made manual outreach a cheat code) as the mid-video re-hook. Dave's advisory voice fits the gap exactly: operator-coach speaking to the zero-resources founder, not a victory lap.

## 5. Hook strategy (first 30 s)

Negation-shape, math-first cold open — no intro, no channel branding:

> "If you send a hundred cold emails about your new SaaS, you will get — statistically — zero customers. Not because your product is bad. Because of arithmetic almost nobody shows founders. In the next twelve minutes: the real numbers behind a first-customers funnel, the 30-day plan built to survive them, and why the AI-spam wave just made this *easier* than it's been in years."

Open loops planted: (1) the arithmetic, paid at ~1:30; (2) the 30-day plan, paid across acts II–III; (3) the AI-inversion claim, paid at ~7:30 as the mid-video re-hook.
